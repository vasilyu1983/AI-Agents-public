# 2. Tactical Decision-Making (Extreme Ownership)

## 2.1 Four Laws of Combat (Operational Version)

Cover & Move → Team-first collaboration
Simple → Reduce cognitive load
Prioritize & Execute → One thing at a time
Decentralize Command → Push decisions down

## 2.2 Prioritize & Execute Pattern

Use in crisis or overload:
Relax, look around
Identify highest-impact problem
Define next action
Communicate clearly
Execute
Repeat

# 6. Difficult Conversations (Decision Scripts)

## 6.1 Alignment Reset (when someone is off-track)

State objective fact: “Here’s what I’ve observed…”
State impact: “Here’s why this matters…”
Ask: “How do you see it?”
Align: “Here’s what success looks like…”
Confirm: “Can we agree on this plan?”

## 6.2 Resetting Expectations

“I need to clarify expectations.”
“Here’s the standard required…”
“Here’s where we are currently…”
“What support do you need?”
“Let’s set check-in points.”

# 11. Negotiation (Voss Patterns)

## 11.1 Calibrated Questions

Use to reveal real constraints.

- “How am I supposed to do that?”  
- “What about this is most important to you?”  
- “What’s the biggest challenge you see?”  

## 11.2 Accusation Audit

Before we start, you might think:
• I’m not being flexible
• We’re slowing things down
• We’re not supporting your goals

## 11.3 Mirroring

Repeat last 1–3 words:

- Helps gather more information

# 13. Decision Trees

## 13.1 Should You Escalate?

Is the issue blocking progress?
├─ No → Do not escalate
└─ Yes
↓
Can the team solve it with more context?
├─ Yes → Provide context
└─ No
↓
Is it cross-team or cross-org?
├─ Yes → Escalate
└─ No → Solve within team

---

## 13.2 Is Delegation Appropriate?

Is outcome clear?
├─ No → Clarify first
└─ Yes
↓
Does the person have skill or stretch potential?
├─ No → Train or pair
└─ Yes
↓
Is failure safe?
├─ No → Keep ownership
└─ Yes → Delegate

---

# 14. Anti-Patterns

- AVOID: Giving unclear direction  
- AVOID: Delegating tasks instead of outcomes  
- AVOID: Avoiding conflict  
- AVOID: Withholding feedback  
- AVOID: Micromanagement  
- AVOID: Decision by consensus for everything  
- AVOID: Escalating prematurely  

---

# 15. Definition of Done (Leadership Decision)

A decision or leadership action is **complete** when:

- [ ] Clearly documented  
- [ ] Owner assigned  
- [ ] Criteria for success defined  
- [ ] Timeline set  
- [ ] Risks acknowledged  
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled
